UPA: UV Space Visible / Invisible Region

For an $N_x \times N_y$ UPA, the ideal DFT grid spans $(u,v) \in [-0.5, 0.5]^2$. The physically realizable visible space is the circle $u^2 + v^2 \le 0.25$ (radius 0.5). When $N_x$ or $N_y > 3$, corner grid points fall into the invisible space where no physical probe angles exist.

Green: visible (inside circle); Red: invisible (outside circle).

Near-Field Telescopic Multi-Probe Anechoic Chamber (TMPAC)

To transcend the physical "invisible space" limitations of large-scale Uniform Planar Arrays (UPAs) in the far-field, the TMPAC leverages spherical wavefront curvature. By dynamically adjusting the radial distances via linear actuators, the TMPAC synthesizes a well-conditioned propagation matrix.

The continuous radial tuning is driven by our Hybrid Relaxed-Discrete Optimization (HRDO) algorithm, utilizing an inverse-radial gradient flow to prevent vanishing gradients at large distances.
